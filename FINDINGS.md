# Key Findings — US Retail Sales (Census Bureau MRTS)

## Scale of US Retail
- **2023 total:** $8.50 trillion in US retail sales
- **Growth vs 2019:** +28.2% despite pandemic disruption

## The COVID Crash and Recovery
- April 2020: -23.2% crash (worst single-month drop in survey history)
- Recovery: returned to pre-pandemic trend in **just 4 months**
- Stimulus checks and supply chain shifts drove explosive bounce-back

## The E-Commerce Transformation (Most Important Finding)
Normal e-commerce adoption: ~1 percentage point per year
COVID Q2 2020: +5.5 percentage points in a single quarter
**= 5.5 years of normal adoption compressed into 12 weeks**

This shift was **permanent**. The e-commerce share never returned to baseline.

## Seasonal Pattern
- December seasonality index: +30% above the average month
- Consistent across all 5 years analyzed
- Retail businesses must plan for 30% December surge

## Data Source
US Census Bureau — Monthly Retail Trade Survey (MRTS)
API: https://api.census.gov/data/timeseries/eits/mrts
Released monthly, public domain government data.
